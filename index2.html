About

The development of sensing and communication technologies turned vehicles and their users into ubiquitous footprints of their mobility patterns. Given the variety and accessibility of information and infrastructure capabilities, ubiquitous computing is considered as key enabler for context-awareness and decision-making in transportation improving user experience. Therefore, it is important to identify opportunities and challenges which ubiquitous technologies can bring to transportation domain, how to tackle them to promote best methods and practices to improve mobility sustainability on large urban areas.
The International Workshop on Ubiquitous Transportation aims to gather professionals from industry and academia focused on ITS (Intelligent Transportation Systems) and ubiquitous computing research to discuss the latest scientific results and practical use cases. The main objective of the workshop is to identify the opportunities and challenges, while discussing best methods and practices of deploying ubiquitous computing technologies across transportation domain.








Topics of interest

Authors are invited to submit original unpublished manuscripts that demonstrate current research, methods, and use cases in using ubiquitous computing technologies in transportation domain.
Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

Data processing (data acquisition, x2x, data fusion, data analytics)
Sensing, Crowdsourcing, mobile computing
Context- and situation-awareness
Context modelling, reasoning, decision-making
Personalization


Methods and algorithms

Machine learning and Data Mining
Statistical analysis
Privacy and Security
Visualization


Real world applications and experiments

Transportation services (road/rail/airline/intercity transportation)
Intelligent transportation systems, Co-operative ITS
Green (eco-, sustainable, safe) driving
Autonomous driving








Paper submission

All the submitted articles must present original research and experience, not published or submitted anywhere else. All submissions should follow SIGCHI Extended Abstract Format and not exceed 8 pages. The templates are available at https://github.com/sigchi/Documents-Formats. All the submissions will be reviewed by at least 2 reviewers.

Proceedings

All the accepted articles will be included in the ACM Digital Library and supplemental proceedings of the UbiComp 2016.
